About GRA

Our History

The Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA) was established on 1st December 2009 by the Ghana Revenue Authority Act 2009, Act 791. It was formed from the merger of three separate revenue agencies: the Customs, Excise and Preventive Service (CEPS), the Internal Revenue Service (IRS), and the Value Added Tax Service (VATS), along with the Revenue Agencies Governing Board (RAGB).

The merger was initiated to ensure the efficient administration of tax laws, eliminate duplication of functions, reduce costs, and increase domestic revenue mobilisation to support national development.

Today, GRA administers and enforces laws relating to revenue collection, including income tax, value added tax (VAT), excise duties, customs and various levies to fund Ghana's national budget and development agenda.

Types of Taxes in Ghana

Ghana has various categories of taxes administered by the Ghana Revenue Authority. Below are the main types of taxes that individuals and businesses are required to pay.

💼

Income Tax

Tax levied on income earned by individuals and businesses. Rates are progressive (0%–35%) for individuals.

• Personal Income Tax (PIT)
• Corporate Income Tax (CIT) – 25%
• Small Business Tax
🛒

Value Added Tax (VAT)

Consumption tax on goods and services. Standard rate is 15%. Includes NHIL (2.5%) and GETFund Levy (2.5%).

• VAT Standard (15%)
• VAT Flat Rate (3%)
• NHIL & GETFund
👥

PAYE (Pay As You Earn)

Monthly tax deducted from employee salaries by employers and remitted to GRA. Due by the 15th of each month.

• Monthly employer obligation
• Progressive rate 0%–35%
• Includes employee relief
✂️

Withholding Tax

Tax deducted at source before payment is made to the recipient. Rates vary (3%–20%) depending on the nature of payment.

• Rent (8%), Dividends (8%)
• Interest (8%), Royalties (15%)
• Professional fees (20%)
📱

Electronic Transfer Levy (E-Levy)

1% levy on electronic money transfers above GHS 100 per day. Introduced in 2022 to widen the tax net.

• Mobile Money transfers
• Bank to mobile transfers
• Merchant payments
🚢

Customs & Excise Duties

Taxes on imported and exported goods. Rates vary based on the Harmonised System (HS) code of goods.

• Import Duty (0–35%)
• Export Levy
• Excise Duty on alcohol & tobacco
🚗

Vehicle Income Tax (VIT)

Quarterly tax paid by commercial vehicle operators. Based on vehicle type and capacity.

• Taxis: GHS 12/quarter
• Minibuses: GHS 25/quarter
• Trucks: GHS 48/quarter
🏠

Property Rate & Gift Tax

Tax on immovable property and gifts received. Capital Gains Tax (CGT) of 15% applies on disposal of chargeable assets.

• Gift Tax (15%)
• Capital Gains (15%)
• Property Transfer Tax

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a TIN and how do I get one?▼
A Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN) is a unique number assigned to every taxpayer. To get one, visit any GRA office or register online at gra.gov.gh. You need your Ghana Card (National ID), proof of address, and for businesses: business registration documents.
How do I register my business for VAT?▼
You must register for VAT if your annual turnover exceeds GHS 200,000. Visit the nearest GRA office or apply online. You will need your business TIN, business registration certificate, and proof of address.
When are PAYE returns due?▼
PAYE returns must be filed and payments made by the 15th of the month following the payroll month. For example, January PAYE is due by 15th February.
What is the deadline for annual income tax returns?▼
Individual income tax returns are due by 30th April each year for the preceding year. Corporate returns are due within 4 months after the end of the accounting year.
Can I file my returns online?▼
Yes! GRA provides an online e-filing platform at taxpayers.gra.gov.gh. You can file all returns including PAYE, VAT, income tax, and withholding tax online.
What payment methods does GRA accept?▼
GRA accepts payments via: Mobile Money (MTN, Vodafone, AirtelTigo), bank transfers, GRA Pay Portal, and at any commercial bank or GRA office. Always use your TIN or Invoice Number as payment reference.
Can I pay my taxes in installments?▼
Yes, GRA allows installment payments for large tax liabilities. You must apply in writing to the Commissioner of your tax office and a payment plan will be agreed upon.
What happens if I file late?▼
Late filing attracts a penalty. For individuals: GHS 500 or 3 times the tax payable. For companies: GHS 500 per month or part thereof. Interest also accrues at 5% above the Bank of Ghana monetary policy rate.
What is the penalty for not registering for tax?▼
Failure to register for tax when required attracts a penalty of GHS 5,000 for individuals and GHS 10,000 for companies. Criminal prosecution may also follow for persistent non-compliance.
Is there a penalty waiver programme?▼
GRA periodically runs amnesty/waiver programmes where taxpayers can clear arrears with reduced or waived penalties. Check the GRA website or contact your nearest tax office for current programmes.
